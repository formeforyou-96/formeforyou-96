Plastic Recycling Crisis: What You Should Know

1. Chemicals Concern Overhaul Urgently Needed to Fix Failed Plastic Recycling Systems

Source: Reuters
Summary: Only 9% of all plastic ever produced has been recycled, with most ending up in landfills or being incinerated. The article highlights the need for systemic reforms and stricter regulations throughout the plastic production and recycling process.

2. Recycling Plastic is Practically Impossible — and the Problem is Getting Worse

Source: NPR
Summary: Greenpeace reports that only about 5% of plastic waste in the United States is actually recycled. Most plastics are either landfilled or burned, calling into question the viability of recycling as a solution to plastic pollution.

3. Plastic Producers Knew Recycling Was Not Viable but Promoted It Anyway, Report Reveals

Source: The Guardian
Summary: A new report finds that plastic producers have long been aware that recycling would not be a sustainable solution, yet they continued promoting it to the public, misleading consumer perceptions about the effectiveness of recycling.

5 Easy Ways to Reduce Plastic Use in Daily Life

  • Use Reusable Shopping Bags: Bring your own reusable bags or eco-bags instead of using disposable plastic bags when shopping. Save dozens of plastic bags every year.
  • Carry a Tumbler or Personal Cup: Use your own tumbler or cup when buying drinks at cafes — some cafes even offer discounts!
  • Choose Products with Minimal Packaging: Select products with simple or recyclable packaging when shopping for groceries or household items. Zero-waste stores are also a great option.
  • Use Reusable Utensils and Items: Replace disposable utensils, straws, and forks with reusable ones made of stainless steel, silicone, or bamboo.
  • Recycle Properly: Clean plastics thoroughly before recycling. Contaminated plastics often end up in landfills instead of being recycled.
